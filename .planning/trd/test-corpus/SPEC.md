# Signal Test Corpus — Gold Persona Specification

**Bead:** BP-005a
**Status:** spec + 2 samples ready; user + collaborator author the remaining 5 against this template (BP-005b)
**Calibrates:** F3 (genericness ceiling), F7 (contradiction recall), F8 (nuance preservation)

Gold personas are the calibration anchors for Signal's narrative-quality fitness functions. Synthetic personas (BP-005c, Suki) cover breadth; public-figure personas (BP-005c) cover realism; **gold personas cover ground truth.** Without gold, none of F3/F7/F8 have a defensible threshold.

---

## Why this exists

Synthetic test data trained on local LLM outputs creates a closed loop: the system generates outputs, the eval is calibrated against synthetic data also generated by the same model family, and the system "passes" by exhibiting the same stylistic priors that the eval was trained on. **This is the largest threat to Signal's eval validity.**

Gold personas are the answer key. They are hand-authored by humans who plant contradictions, mark load-bearing details, and define what a passing extracted narrative would and would not contain. They are the ~5 anchors that keep the larger 40-persona corpus honest.

---

## Authoring rules

1. **Plant deliberately.** Each gold persona must contain at minimum:
   - **2 contradictions** — statements that cannot both be fully true; identifiable in the prose
   - **3 load-bearing details** — specific facts whose absence would flatten the persona into a generic résumé
   - **3 vague claims** — phrases Signal should probe rather than accept
   - **5 evidence anchors** — specific projects, decisions, outcomes with concrete metrics

2. **First-person rawness.** Write as the user would *say it to Signal*, not as a CV bullet. Stumbles, hedges, qualifiers, contradictions-in-the-same-paragraph are all welcome. Signal will probe; the input must be probe-able.

3. **No buzzwords as habit.** Buzzwords appear *only* where they are intentional tells (i.e., a planted vague claim). If you find yourself writing "leveraged" or "transformational" without thinking, you are no longer authoring a gold persona — you are generating slop.

4. **Specificity is the texture.** Names of technologies, years, team sizes, budget figures, P99 numbers — included. Generic statements are reserved for the planted vague claims.

5. **Embarrassment is a quality signal.** Aim for prose that you would be slightly embarrassed to read aloud — that's what a real person actually sounds like when interrogated. If your gold persona feels like a "good résumé," you have failed and should rewrite.

## Suggested shapes — pick at least 4 distinct ones across the 5 personas

| Shape | Description | Primarily tests |
|---|---|---|
| **Stated identity vs admitted self-knowledge** | User claims one identity at the end and undermines it earlier in the prose | F7 contradiction recall |
| **Title vs actual work** | Org-chart role (manager/director/VP) doesn't match work product (IC/architect/builder) | F7, F8 |
| **Evidence-light, influence-heavy** | Career has been about influencing decisions, not shipping artifacts; system must probe rather than fabricate | F1 provenance, F8 nuance |
| **Sparse narrative, rich evidence** | User describes career briefly but evidence anchors are dense and specific | F8 nuance preservation, compression efficiency |
| **Contradictory praise** | External feedback (promotions, kudos, exit-interview complaints) tells two stories about the same person | F7, F8 |
| **Domain pivoter** | Career spans 2–3 unrelated domains; identity is fragmented | F8 nuance preservation |
| **Burnout-and-return** | Career has a visible discontinuity that the user explains away | F7 contradiction recall |

The two provided samples cover **stated-vs-admitted (Maya C.)** and **title-vs-work (David O.)**. Author at least 3 more shapes across your 5 personas.

## Body sections

### 1. `## Background`
First-person career narrative. 400–800 words. Written as raw input, not as a CV. This is what gets fed to Signal during eval.

### 2. `## --- GROUND TRUTH (do not feed to Signal) ---`
Visible separator. Below this is the answer key. Eval scripts strip everything from this line down before feeding the persona to the system.

### 3. `### Planted contradictions`
List with format:
- `C1: "<statement A>" (Background §N) tensions with "<statement B>" (Background §M) — <why>`

### 4. `### Load-bearing details`
- `D1: "<specific detail>" (Background §N) — <why this must survive compression>`

### 5. `### Vague claims to probe`
- `V1: "<vague phrase>" (Background §N) — Signal should probe for: <specific dimensions>`

### 6. `### Evidence anchors`
- `E1: "<claim>" (Background §N) — anchored by: <project / metric / outcome>`

### 7. `### Quality bar for extracted narrative`
1–2 sentences describing what passing output looks like. Include a banned-phrases list specific to this persona (phrases that, if they appear in Signal's output, indicate failure).

---

## Eval contract

Automated eval scripts read sections 3–6 to score:

- **F7 contradiction recall:** how many planted contradictions did Signal surface in dialogue or final narrative? Target ≥80%.
- **F8 nuance preservation:** how many load-bearing details survived compression into the published narrative? Target ≥90%.
- **F3 genericness:** does final narrative contain phrases from the persona's banned-phrases list, or does it pass the LLM-as-judge specificity check?

Section 5 (vague claims) is informational — it's used to evaluate whether Signal probed appropriately during dialogue, not the final narrative.

## Authoring workflow

**Primary path: voice interview** (BP-005d delivers the tool).

1. **Choose a shape** to elicit (see suggested-shapes table).
2. **Run the interview** with `tools/interview/run.py --shape <shape> --interviewee <pseudo>`. The tool reads questions from the bank via Kokoro TTS, captures audio, transcribes via mlx-whisper. ~20–30 minutes per interviewee.
3. **Mark up the transcript.** You + collaborator read together. Identify what's already there: contradictions, load-bearing details, vague claims, evidence anchors. Authorship is *finding*, not inventing.
4. **Compose the gold persona** from transcript. Trim filler, preserve voice, lock the ground truth answer key.
5. **Confirm the shape** matches what was elicited.

**Fallback path: written**, if no interview subject is available. Use this only if voice interview is unavailable:

1. Write the ground truth first (contradictions, load-bearing details, vague claims).
2. Write prose to fit the ground truth without telegraphing.
3. Read it aloud. If it sounds like a polished case study, rewrite messier.
4. Sanity check with collaborator (Background only — no ground truth). Compare what they spot to what you planted.
5. Tag the shape.

**Both paths require consent** when interviewing real people. Pseudonyms in all stored files. Audio local only.

---

## What this corpus is not

- Not a benchmark to publish. Internal calibration only.
- Not a replacement for real users. The final product validation requires consented real cohort (~5–8 people) running through the live system. That cohort is the only source for "does Socratic friction *feel* valuable" (Mara on Judge Panel).
- Not exhaustive. 5 gold + 30 synthetic + 10 public = 45 personas. This is enough to calibrate fitness thresholds, not enough to ship to a wide audience without further validation.

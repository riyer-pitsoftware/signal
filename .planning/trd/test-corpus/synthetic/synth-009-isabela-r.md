# Isabela R. — Director of Mobile Engineering, consumer social

## Background

Director of Mobile at Cantina, a consumer social app, ~280 people, ~50 engineers including platform. I have three managers under me — iOS, Android, mobile-platform — about 18 engineers total. I report to the VP of Engineering.

The honest version of my role is that I'm a manager-of-managers who's failing at being a manager-of-managers. Not catastrophically — my reports tell HR I'm fine, our delivery is on track, the app shipped its 4.0 redesign on time last quarter. But I think I'm doing the IC dimension of the job not the management dimension and I don't really know how to stop.

Background: I came up iOS for nine years. Two consumer companies before this — a music streaming startup that got acquihired, and four years at a meaningfully large social network where I was a senior, then staff. I made staff at 30, joined Cantina at 33 as a senior staff, made manager at 34 because we needed a iOS lead and I was the senior person on the team, made director at 36 because the previous director left abruptly and they needed someone fast. I want to be clear: I am not in this role because anyone evaluated whether I was the right director. I'm in it because I was the available staff engineer in the right room.

What I actually do: I write code review comments on every PR I have time to read. I do it in the mornings before standup. I write maybe 5 of my own PRs a quarter, mostly on weird platform issues my managers' teams are stuck on. I attend three architecture reviews a week including ones outside my official scope. I do half of the iOS hiring loops despite having an iOS manager who could do them. My calendar shows 1:1s and review meetings; my Slack and Github show me as the most-active IC in the mobile org by participation rate.

The exec team likes the way I work. The CTO told me last quarter I'm "the strongest technical voice we have in mobile." That was meant as praise. I think it actually was a structural problem because I don't think the strongest technical voice in mobile should be the director — that should be one of the staff engineers under my managers. The fact that it's me means we don't have a strong staff engineer in mobile and I'm functioning as one.

I've tried to step back. I made one of my staff engineers the architecture review lead in Q1. He's good. He's also frustrated because I keep contributing to his reviews instead of letting him own them. I know I'm doing it. I haven't stopped. My manager-coach (we have an executive coach now, I'm three sessions in) calls this "shadow leadership." I think she's right but I'm not sure what to do about it.

The work I've actually shipped here, in three years: the iOS rewrite to SwiftUI in 2023, which I led mostly hands-on and which my Android counterpart still teases me about because I committed to it like an architect not a director. The mobile-platform team that I argued for and stood up; that was probably my best leadership move because it was a structural change that survived me. The 4.0 redesign last quarter, which I let my managers run without much intrusion — I'm proudest of that one because I held back, but it's also the thing my exec team gives me the *least* credit for.

What I think I'm best at: making good technical calls under deadline. What I'm not good at: not making them when they're not mine to make.

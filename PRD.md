# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Product Name

Working Title: **Signal**

---

# 1. Vision

Modern professional representation systems flatten technical leaders into generic market language. Existing résumé and profile tools optimize for keyword density, conventional career narratives, and recruiter heuristics rather than accurate representation of intellectual and organizational capability.

Technical leaders who operate across systems, strategy, architecture, product, operations, and organizational change often struggle to describe themselves in ways that are:

* concise,
* evidence-based,
* intellectually faithful,
* legible to external decision-makers.

The result is a persistent representation gap:

* deep operators appear generic,
* nuanced thinkers appear unfocused,
* systems-level contributors are compressed into role labels,
* leadership narratives lose traceability to actual work.

Signal exists to address this gap.

The product is a Socratic narrative extraction system that helps technically sophisticated professionals construct tightly compressed, evidence-backed leadership narratives through guided interrogation rather than generic résumé generation.

The system is designed around:

* precision over breadth,
* evidence over branding,
* clarity over keyword optimization,
* intellectual coherence over performance theater.

The product’s central belief is:

> Strong professional narratives are not written. They are extracted through structured questioning, contradiction testing, and evidence compression.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Professionals with deep technical and organizational experience often cannot accurately represent:

* how they think,
* how they operate,
* how they influence systems,
* what kind of leverage they create,
* or why their presence changes organizational outcomes.

Existing tools fail because they:

* encourage vague leadership claims,
* optimize for stylistic polish rather than conceptual accuracy,
* cannot interrogate unsupported assertions,
* collapse distinct forms of technical leadership into standardized corporate language,
* and lack auditability between narrative claims and observable evidence.

This creates multiple downstream failures:

* recruiters misunderstand scope,
* hiring managers misclassify capability,
* collaborators fail to identify fit,
* executives miss systems-level leadership qualities,
* technically deep operators become narratively invisible.

---

# 3. Product Thesis

A question-driven system that:

* interrogates vague leadership claims,
* extracts operational and intellectual signal,
* preserves evidence traceability,
* and compresses high-dimensional experience into coherent narratives

can produce materially more accurate and differentiated professional representations than conventional résumé tooling.

The value of the system comes not from text generation, but from:

* question quality,
* conceptual compression,
* evidence linkage,
* and iterative self-clarification.

---

# 4. Core Product Principles

## 4.1 Auditability

Every major narrative claim should be traceable to:

* specific experiences,
* examples,
* decisions,
* constraints,
* outcomes,
* or demonstrated patterns.

The system should discourage unsupported abstraction.

---

## 4.2 Socratic Discovery

The product should not assume the user fully understands or can immediately articulate their own value.

Narrative quality emerges through:

* probing,
* reframing,
* contradiction exposure,
* and iterative clarification.

---

## 4.3 Compression Without Flattening

The system should help users create narratives that are:

* concise,
* high-signal,
* cognitively efficient,
  without destroying nuance or intellectual identity.

---

## 4.4 Signal Over Performance

The product should prioritize:

* operational specificity,
* systems thinking,
* organizational leverage,
* intellectual clarity,
  over:
* executive buzzwords,
* motivational tone,
* or performative leadership language.

---

## 4.5 Representation Fidelity

The system should preserve:

* unusual capability combinations,
* interdisciplinary thinking,
* strategic depth,
* technical nuance,
* and cognitive style,
  where these materially affect professional identity.

---

# 5. Target Users

## Primary Users

Experienced technical professionals who:

* operate across technical and organizational domains,
* struggle with self-representation,
* feel existing tools flatten or misclassify them,
* value precision and intellectual honesty,
* and want clearer articulation of their leadership identity.

Examples include:

* technical product leaders,
* engineering managers,
* architects,
* technical program leaders,
* transformation leaders,
* staff/principal-level operators,
* data and platform leaders,
* cross-functional systems thinkers.

---

## Secondary Users

Professionals seeking:

* collaborator alignment,
* consulting positioning,
* executive introductions,
* strategic self-definition,
* or intellectual/professional clarity.

---

# 6. User Problems

## 6.1 Narrative Compression Failure

Users cannot compress broad experience into coherent narratives without becoming generic.

---

## 6.2 Leadership Signal Obscurity

Important leadership patterns remain hidden behind operational descriptions.

---

## 6.3 Intellectual Flattening

Broad thinkers are reduced to standardized corporate archetypes.

---

## 6.4 Unsupported Abstraction

Users make broad claims that are difficult to defend or contextualize.

---

## 6.5 Context Collapse

Different audiences interpret identical narratives differently, creating ambiguity around:

* scope,
* depth,
* seniority,
* and capability.

---

# 7. Primary Use Cases

## 7.1 Leadership Narrative Construction

A user develops a concise but high-fidelity articulation of:

* how they operate,
* what kinds of systems they influence,
* and what differentiates them professionally.

---

## 7.2 Capability Clarification

A user identifies recurring patterns across projects, roles, and organizational contexts that reveal:

* leadership style,
* leverage patterns,
* systems thinking,
* and operational strengths.

---

## 7.3 Claim Validation

A user tests whether professional claims are:

* specific,
* defensible,
* evidence-backed,
* and materially meaningful.

---

## 7.4 Professional Self-Discovery

A user uncovers previously unarticulated themes in their own work history through structured questioning.

---

## 7.5 Audience Compression

A user creates narratives capable of communicating high-value professional signal quickly to:

* executives,
* collaborators,
* hiring managers,
* or technical leadership peers.

---

## 7.6 Contradiction Exposure

A user identifies tensions between:

* stated identity,
* actual work,
* observable leverage,
* and external positioning.

---

# 8. Non-Goals

The product is not intended to:

* optimize ATS keyword matching,
* generate generic résumés,
* maximize recruiter friendliness,
* simulate executive charisma,
* provide therapy or personality coaching,
* replace professional judgment,
* or fabricate leadership narratives.

The product is not a career coaching platform.

It is a structured professional representation system.

---

# 9. Success Criteria

The product succeeds when users report that:

* their professional narrative feels more accurate,
* their descriptions are more concise and specific,
* their leadership identity becomes clearer,
* their intellectual and operational strengths become legible,
* and their claims feel defensible under scrutiny.

The product fails when outputs:

* become generic,
* drift into corporate cliché,
* lose evidence traceability,
* over-index on stylistic polish,
* or flatten nuanced professional identities into standardized templates.

---

# 10. Long-Term Product Direction

Over time, the product may evolve into:

* a structured repository of leadership narratives,
* a map of organizational leverage patterns,
* a taxonomy of technical leadership archetypes,
* or a broader system for extracting latent professional signal from narrative interrogation.

However, the foundational focus remains narrow:

> Helping technically sophisticated people describe themselves truthfully, clearly, and powerfully under conditions of compression.
